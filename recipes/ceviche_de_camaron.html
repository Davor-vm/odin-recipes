Shrimp Ceviche

Description

Shrimp ceviche is a fresh dish typical of the Latin American coast. It's prepared with shrimp cooked in lemon juice, mixed with onion, tomato, cilantro, and sometimes chili or avocado.

It's light and refreshing and is often served with tostadas, crackers, or fried plantains, depending on the region.

Ingredients

  • 2 lb shelled, headless and shucked shrimp
  • 1 lb of finely diced tomato
  • A bunch of cilantro, finely chopped
  • 2 or 3 cucumbers diced
  • 1 Large onion finely chopped
  • 2 jalapeños peppers, deseeded, finely chopped
  • Salt
  • Pepper
  • Hot sauce
  • 2 Large Avocados

Steps

  1. Clean the shrimp and chop them into medium-sized pieces.
  2. Pour over the lemon juice and let the shrimp sit for 40 minutes to 1 hour allowing them to cook (until they turn white and medium-reddish).
  3. Once the shrimp is cooked, add the onion, tomato, cilantro, cucumber, and jalapeños
  4. Season with salt and pepper to taste
  5. Serve with Tostadas Cardenas or crackers and hot sauce (Tapatío, Valentina, or Huichol).

    6. Optional

  6. Add diced avocado to the mix or slices on top of the tostadas.
